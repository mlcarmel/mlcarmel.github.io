About Me

Hello! I'm Margaret Carmel. I am the Senior Reporter with BoiseDev.com, a locally owned news start up in Boise, Idaho. I cover city government, politics, the Idaho Legislature, housing, homelessness, growth and economics. Prior to coming to BoiseDev, I was the Boise City Hall Reporter at the Idaho Press and I also worked at The News & Advance in Lynchburg, Virginia. In Virginia, I covered business, economic development, two rural counties and then eventually Lychburg City Hall. I earned my undergraduate degree in Broadcast Journalism with minors in International Social Justice and Middle Eastern Studies from Virginia Commonwealth University in 2016. My favorite past times include reading, writing and exploring Idaho's wilderness.

Another aspect of my job is covering the day-to-day business of governing when difficult problems arise in Boise. This article is the latest in my reporting on downtown business complaints about "The Cruise", a tradition where people in high performance cars drive circles around Boise, and how the government is trying to create effective rules to stop the situation without infringing on someone's rights.

The Cruise has been happening in Downtown Boise for decades, but the city wants to crack down on noise, litering and speeding.
